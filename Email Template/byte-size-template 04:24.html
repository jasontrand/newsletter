A Powerful Duo: Kafka and Schema Registry

Apache Kafka has become increasingly popular due to its scalable, robust, and fault-tolerant publish-subscribe architecture, evolving into one of the fastest-growing open-source projects since its development by LinkedIn in 2010. Now utilized by thousands of organizations, Kafka's architecture is foundational to many data-driven applications. This introduction focuses on Schema Registry, a crucial component that enhances data governance within Kafka systems. For those interested in exploring Kafka further, "Kafka: The Definitive Guide" and case studies from The New York Times and Netflix provide valuable insights into its capabilities and applications.

Schema Registry is a simple concept but it’s really powerful in enforcing data governance within your Kafka architecture. Schemas reside outside of your Kafka cluster, only the schema ID resides in your Kafka, hence making schema registry a critical component of your infrastructure. If the schema registry is not available, it will break producers and consumers. So it is always a best practice to ensure your schema registry is highly available.

GitHub Pages: An Introductory Tutorial

GitHub is a premier platform widely utilized by organizations and individuals for hosting and version controlling source code repositories, leveraging Git software to enhance collaboration and track code changes efficiently. Among its innovative features, GitHub Pages stands out as a powerful tool that enables users to host static websites directly from their repositories, making them accessible to the public or restricted to internal teams. This functionality is ideal for creating project documentation, personal portfolios, and landing pages, allowing users to launch websites in just minutes with a user-friendly URL format (https://.github.io).

How Airbnb Powers Personalization With 1M Events Per Second

Airbnb’s User Signals Platform (USP) is a robust, production-grade system that plays a crucial role in delivering personalized experiences on one of the largest travel platforms globally. Currently, the USP processes over 1 million events per second and supports more than 100 Flink jobs in production, handling 70,000 queries per second on its service layer. This platform is integral to Airbnb's core infrastructure, with contributions from various teams that define signal parameters, segments, and use cases. Despite its impressive capabilities, the USP team recognizes opportunities for further enhancement, particularly in managing asynchronous computing. Future plans include advancing pipeline orchestration, implementing pluggable execution backends, and developing comprehensive compute graphs. While the technology behind the USP, such as Flink and Kafka, is noteworthy, its success is largely attributed to strategic design choices. These include adopting an append-only data model for resilience, simplifying complexity for developers, and prioritizing operational resilience. Ultimately, the platform's philosophy emphasizes that real-time data must be actionable, enabling teams across Airbnb to define signals and create personalized experiences without requiring deep expertise in streaming technologies.

The Great Migration from MongoDB to PostgreSQL

Infisical has experienced significant growth over the past year, now processing over 50 million secrets daily for application configuration and secure data management. To accommodate this increasing demand, the platform recently underwent a substantial migration from MongoDB to PostgreSQL. This transition was essential due to limitations encountered with MongoDB, particularly regarding transaction support and usability challenges for self-hosting users. The migration involved careful planning and execution, including the creation of new database schemas and the rewriting of queries, ultimately aimed at enhancing the platform's performance and accessibility for organizations. This article shares the decision-making process and experiences behind this critical database migration.
